# Polymarket C++ Arbitrage Detector — Situation Brief

## Project context

Repo: `/home/joaop/PyResearch/polymarket/cpp`
A C++ arbitrage detector for Polymarket CLOB. Subscribes to the
`ws-subscriptions-clob.polymarket.com` WebSocket feed, parses book/price_change/bbo
events with simdjson, maintains per-contract orderbooks, and runs arbitrage checks
on every book update. Outputs CSV logs + a live summary banner with latency
histograms and a "paper trading" P&L.

Key files:
- `src/main.cpp`         — pipeline, frame loop, dispatch
- `src/websocket.cpp`    — Boost.Beast WS client (zero-copy into simdjson pad buffer)
- `src/parser.hpp`       — simdjson event parsing
- `src/orderbook.cpp`    — book apply_book / apply_price_change / apply_best_bid_ask
- `src/arbitrage.cpp`    — arb detection (taker + maker, single-contract + group)
- `src/logger.cpp`       — CSV writers, latency/edge tracking, summary banner
- `src/types.hpp`        — Price/Size typedefs, Contract/Orderbook/Config structs
- `config.strategy.json` — 10 contracts across Fed-April, WBA-Millwall, Famalicao groups

Pipeline latency is ~400 µs end-to-end (t0 WS recv → t3 arb checked), all threads
CPU-pinned (receiver=2, parser=4, logger=6). This part is solid and well within
the sub-ms target.

## What I was asked to check

The user ran 3 × 5-second benchmarks against `config.strategy.json` and pasted the
summary output into `outputs.txt`. The banner reported:

    Run 1: Messages=2, Opportunities=10, Paper P&L=$9833.11, Volume=$40810
    Run 2: Messages=1, Opportunities=0,  Paper P&L=$0,       Volume=$0
    Run 3: Messages=2, Opportunities=5,  Paper P&L=$10181.56, Volume=$30760

The user asked "it seems that it works right?" — I went into the code and the
associated CSV/replay logs to verify whether those P&L numbers represent a
real profit-making mechanism. They do not.

## Bottom line

- Pipeline (WS / parse / book / latency): **working as designed, fast and correct.**
- Taker arbitrage detection (`BUY_BOTH`, `SELL_BOTH`, `BUY_GROUP_YES`,
  `SELL_GROUP_YES`, `BUY_NO_SELL_OTHERS`, `SELL_NO_BUY_OTHERS`): **math is correct.**
  On these specific market states, it correctly reports **negative edges** — there
  is no real arbitrage available. E.g. `BUY_BOTH best raw=-60 bps`,
  `BUY_GROUP_YES best raw=-8670 bps`.
- Paper P&L number: **not a real P&L.** The entire positive number comes from the
  maker-arb categories, which are computed under assumptions that do not hold on
  the markets being tested. And even that fictional number is inflated ~5× by a
  per-frame multi-count bug.

So the engine is a working arbitrage **scanner**. It has not found any actual
arbitrage yet. The "profit-making mechanism" in the output is an accounting
artifact.

## Concrete evidence from `logs/arb_strategy.csv` + `logs/replay_strategy.csv`

### Bug 1 — One market state produces 5 "opportunities" all counted independently

Frame `11034150942619` in `logs/replay_strategy.csv` lines 23-26 has this book
for the Fed-April group (4 legs, exhaustive):

    Contract           yes_bid(size)   yes_ask(size)   no_bid   no_ask(size)
    FED-DEC-50BPS      4 (575,837)     215 (200)       785      996
    FED-DEC-25BPS      10 (69)         347 (5)         653      990
    FED-NO-CHANGE      974 (5,662)     980 (5,494)     20       26 (5,662)
    FED-INC-25BPS      7 (66,087)      325 (2,500)     675      993

`logs/arb_strategy.csv` lines 17-21 then emits **5 separate opportunities** for
that single frame, all with `theoretical_pnl=1874.12` each:

    row 17: MAKER_SELL_GROUP_YES                              (whole group)
    row 18: MAKER_BUY_NO_SELL_OTHERS  focal=FED-DEC-50BPS
    row 19: MAKER_BUY_NO_SELL_OTHERS  focal=FED-DEC-25BPS
    row 20: MAKER_BUY_NO_SELL_OTHERS  focal=FED-NO-CHANGE
    row 21: MAKER_BUY_NO_SELL_OTHERS  focal=FED-INC-25BPS

In `main.cpp:466-474`, `enqueue_output` iterates every opportunity in the
`ArbCheckOutput` and pushes each to the opportunity queue unconditionally.
In `logger.cpp:273` each one adds to `cumulative_pnl_`. So one synthetic
book state booked **5 × $1874.12 = $9370.60**, which is ~95% of Run 1's $9833
cumulative total. In reality those 5 "strategies" share positions and collapse
to **at most one** executable trade.

### Bug 2 — Maker-arb edge is fictional on dead markets

The MAKER_* strategies post a passive order one tick inside the spread and
assume you simultaneously hit the other legs at their current best bid/ask.

`arbitrage.cpp:582-623` (`MAKER_SELL_GROUP_YES`) picks FED-DEC-25BPS as passive
(`inside_sell = ask - 1 = 346`) and uses others' best bids:

    total_proceeds = 346 + 4 + 974 + 7 = 1331
    raw_edge = 1331 - 1000 = 331 thou = 3310 bps

That's the 33.1% "edge" in the banner. What it actually requires:

1. Post a limit sell at $0.346 on FED-DEC-25BPS, which sits **$0.337 above the
   current best bid of $0.010** on a dead market.
2. Someone crosses that 337 bps spread to hit your order.
3. While that happens, the three other quotes (FED-DEC-50BPS bid=$0.004,
   FED-NO-CHANGE bid=$0.974, FED-INC-25BPS bid=$0.007) remain available.

None of those are realistic. In particular, FED-NO-CHANGE's 974 yes_bid @ size
5662 and 26 no_ask @ size 5662 are almost certainly the **same LP posting both
sides of the same position** (identical size, complementary prices summing to
exactly 1000). Hitting their 974 bid with size 5662 wipes their quote and the
"arb" disappears.

The detector knows there's no taker arb on this frame — `SELL_GROUP_YES best
raw=-50 bps` shows the real sum-of-bids = 995 < 1000. The 3310 bps number comes
purely from the maker-inside-spread assumption.

### Bug 3 — `size_usd` is mislabeled (it's shares, not dollars)

`types.hpp:13` defines `using Size = uint32_t;` with a comment "share counts
truncated to whole numbers". `arbitrage.cpp:94` assigns that Size into
`opp.size_usd`. `logger.cpp:274` sums it into `cumulative_vol_` and
`logger.cpp:462` prints `Volume: $%.0f`.

So `Volume: $40810` is actually **40,810 shares**, not dollars notional.
(The `theoretical_pnl` is in dollars correctly — `edge_fraction × shares = $` —
but the Volume line has the wrong units/label.)

Real dollar notional would be `sum(size_shares × price_per_share)`, which
nobody computes.

### Bug 4 — Markets under test are dead, not the engine's fault but makes benchmarks useless

- `FAMALICAO-*` (3 contracts) — metadata lookup returns nothing; football game
  was Apr 10 and markets are effectively expired. They never send book events.
- Only 1-2 messages per 5-second run, vs the tens of thousands/s you'd want on
  an active market.
- `Books: 14` across 10 contracts = 7 contracts × 2 sides — the 3 FAMALICAO
  contracts never sent their initial snapshots.
- `WBA-MILLWALL-DRAW` has `yes_bid=999, yes_ask=0` — one-sided locked book,
  probably a stale resting order.
- The FED group is dominated by a single LP on FED-NO-CHANGE; the other three
  legs have near-zero bids and tiny top-of-book sizes.

Run 2 reported $0 because the FED-NO-CHANGE 974@5662 quote was gone during that
5-second window (see `replay_strategy.csv:34-40` where FED-NO-CHANGE shows
`yes_bid=640 size=37`).

## What the engine has correctly detected so far

- Pipeline works, latency is good (sub-ms e2e), CPU pinning engages, reconnect
  logic present, zero-copy into simdjson pad buffer working.
- Taker-arb detection returns correctly-negative edges on dead markets. That's
  the truth of those markets — no trader would execute on them.
- Exhaustive basket auto-detection works: picks up Fed-April as 4-leg and
  WBA-Millwall as 3-leg correctly (FAMALICAO group is skipped because
  metadata failed).

## What needs to be fixed before the P&L number means anything

Priority order — (1) and (2) are mandatory before the paper-trading number
is worth reading at all, (3) and (4) are cleanup.

1. **Emit at most one winner per frame.** In `main.cpp:466-474` the detector
   output is iterated and every opportunity is pushed. Instead, pick the single
   best opportunity per frame (ranked by `net_edge_thou * min_size` or similar)
   and drop the rest, OR deduplicate by the set of actual legs touched. The
   5 MAKER_* variants on the Fed group are all in overlapping position space —
   they shouldn't all be booked.

   Alternative: split `cumulative_pnl_` into *realizable* vs *theoretical* so
   you can still see all variants in CSV but only one counts toward P&L.

2. **Gate or separately track MAKER_* strategies.** They rely on a fill
   assumption that needs a fill-probability model or at minimum a realism
   filter: e.g. require `inside_price` to be within N ticks of `best_bid`, or
   require recent trade volume on the passive leg. Currently nothing prevents
   a MAKER_SELL at `ask - 1 = 346` from booking 3310 bps of fictional edge
   against a `bid = 10` market.

   Simplest first step: keep MAKER_* detection for telemetry/CSV but exclude
   it from `cumulative_pnl_` until a fill model exists. The TAKER paper-P&L
   alone would be an honest number.

3. **Rename `size_usd` → `size_shares`** throughout (`types.hpp:112`,
   `arbitrage.cpp`, `logger.*`, `arb_strategy.csv` header). Recompute the
   banner's `Volume: $X` as dollar notional: `sum(size_shares × price_per_share)`.
   Or just drop it; it's the P&L number that matters.

4. **Deduplicate group+focal overlap.** `MAKER_BUY_NO_SELL_OTHERS` with 4
   different focals is 4 rotations of one strategy family. The detector should
   emit the single best-focal variant per group per frame, not all of them.

## How to validate a fix

The user's memory notes (`/home/joaop/.claude/projects/-home-joaop-PyResearch-polymarket/memory/MEMORY.md`)
already say to "find high-volume crypto 30-min markets for tighter spreads."
Do that first — benchmarking on dead football/fed markets tells you nothing.

Use this API to find candidates:
    https://gamma-api.polymarket.com/markets?closed=false&limit=5&order=volume24hr&ascending=false

Then re-run the benchmark harness:
    SKIP_BUILD=1 LIVE_OUTPUT=1 ./deploy/benchmark_ec2.sh ./config.strategy.json 30

A sanity test after fixes (1) and (2):
- On a real active crypto market, you should see hundreds to thousands of
  messages per 5-second window, not 1-2.
- `Opportunities` should be roughly linear with message count, not a constant
  5-per-frame-multiplier.
- TAKER paper P&L should be small or zero on efficient markets — that is the
  *correct* answer. If it's not small, either there's a real arb (great!) or
  there's still a double-count.
- MAKER paper P&L (if you keep tracking it separately) should be bounded by a
  realistic fill-rate haircut.
